Human Resources
Everything you need to know about how World YMCA supports, develops, and works alongside its people — from your first day to ongoing professional life.
Section Introduction — choose your audience

The Human Resources policy sets out the framework within which World YMCA recruits, supports, develops, and manages its staff. It applies to all permanent and contract employees across the organisation and reflects our commitment to fair, transparent, and values-driven employment practices.

This section covers recruitment and onboarding, contracts and working conditions, performance management, leave entitlements, disciplinary and grievance procedures, and staff wellbeing. All staff are required to familiarise themselves with these policies and apply them consistently in their day-to-day work. Compliance is a condition of employment. Always refer to the current version published here, as it supersedes any earlier documentation.

For permanent and contract staff

This section sets out World YMCA's Human Resources policies, covering recruitment, contracts, performance management, leave, disciplinary procedures, and staff wellbeing. All staff are expected to read and apply these policies as part of their employment. Always refer to the current version published here, as it supersedes any earlier documentation.

Quick reference — all staff

This section explains how World YMCA looks after the people who work here. It covers how we hire, support, and work with our team. These guidelines help make sure everyone is treated fairly and respectfully. Please read the parts that relate to your role with us.

For volunteers and partners
Key Principles
  • 01
    Fair and Transparent Recruitment
    All hiring decisions are based on merit, competence, and organisational need. Selection processes must be consistent, documented, and free from discrimination.
  • 02
    Respect and Dignity at Work
    Every staff member is entitled to a respectful, inclusive, and psychologically safe working environment. Harassment, bullying, or discrimination of any kind is unacceptable.
  • 03
    Performance and Development
    World YMCA is committed to supporting the professional growth of all staff through regular reviews, learning opportunities, and constructive feedback.
  • 04
    Clear and Accessible Procedures
    Disciplinary, grievance, and appeal processes are clearly defined, applied consistently, and accessible to all staff regardless of role or seniority.
  • 05
    Wellbeing as Organisational Responsibility
    Staff health and wellbeing are recognised as integral to organisational effectiveness. World YMCA provides resources and support structures to promote this.
Policy Highlights
Probationary Period
All new employees serve a defined probationary period with structured check-ins and a formal review before confirmation of employment.
Leave Entitlements
Annual leave, sick leave, parental leave, and special leave are outlined in individual contracts and governed by Swiss labour law and organisational policy.
Performance Reviews
Formal performance conversations take place at least annually. Mid-year check-ins are encouraged as standard practice across all departments.
Grievance Procedure
Staff may raise a formal grievance at any time. All complaints are handled confidentially, impartially, and in accordance with the documented procedure.
Conflict of Interest
Staff must disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest to their line manager. Undisclosed conflicts may constitute a disciplinary matter.
Confidentiality
All staff must maintain confidentiality of personal data, organisational information, and sensitive communications, including after employment ends.
Quick Reference — what to do and what to avoid
Please do
  • Complete all onboarding documentation before your start date
  • Raise concerns early through your line manager or the grievance process
  • Keep your personal details and emergency contacts up to date
  • Request leave through the approved process with adequate notice
  • Participate actively in your performance review conversations
Please avoid
  • Sharing confidential personnel information with colleagues or third parties
  • Using informal channels to resolve matters that require a formal procedure
  • Taking on additional employment without first disclosing it to management
  • Ignoring a disciplinary or grievance communication — respond within the stated timeframe
  • Misrepresenting qualifications, experience, or identity at any stage of employment
Frequently Asked Questions
  • Your first point of contact is your line manager. If the concern involves your line manager, you may escalate directly to the Head of HR or use the confidential grievance procedure outlined in the HR policy documents.
  • Secondary employment is not automatically prohibited, but it must be disclosed to your line manager and must not create a conflict of interest or compromise your availability and performance in your primary role.
  • Requests should be submitted with a minimum of two weeks' notice for periods of up to five days, and four weeks' notice for longer periods. Approval is subject to operational needs and is at the discretion of your line manager.
  • You will receive written notification of the concern, be given the opportunity to respond, and may be accompanied by a colleague or staff representative at any formal meeting. The full procedure is available on this portal.

Safeguarding & Duty of Care
Protecting the safety and dignity of every person we work with — especially children, young people, and vulnerable adults — is a shared responsibility we take seriously together.
Section Introduction— choose your audience

Safeguarding is a non-negotiable responsibility for all staff, volunteers, and partners of World YMCA. This section sets out the organisation's duty of care obligations and the procedures that must be followed to protect children, young people, and vulnerable adults in every context in which the organisation operates, including in-person programmes, digital environments, and partner-managed activities.

All staff are required to complete mandatory safeguarding training, understand their reporting obligations, and act immediately on any concern or disclosure. Failure to report a safeguarding concern is a serious breach of policy. Always use the current version of this policy, as procedures may be updated in response to evolving best practice or regulatory requirements.

For permanent and contract staff

This section outlines World YMCA's safeguarding obligations and duty of care responsibilities. All staff must complete safeguarding training, know their reporting obligations, and act on any concern without delay. Safeguarding applies in all programme contexts, including digital and partner-led activities. Always refer to the current version published here.

Quick reference — all staff

Keeping everyone safe is one of the most important things we do at World YMCA. This section explains what we mean by safeguarding and what you should do if you are ever worried about someone's safety or wellbeing. If you see or hear something concerning, please tell a staff member straight away.

For volunteers and partners
Key Principles
  • 01
    Safety is Unconditional
    The safety and wellbeing of children, young people, and vulnerable adults takes precedence over all other considerations, including organisational reputation or convenience.
  • 02
    Mandatory Reporting
    All staff and volunteers have an obligation to report any safeguarding concern, disclosure, or incident through the designated reporting channel without delay.
  • 03
    Safe Recruitment Practices
    All individuals working with children or vulnerable groups must pass a background check and attend safeguarding induction before taking up their role.
  • 04
    Dignity and Respect
    Every individual has the right to be treated with dignity. Abusive, exploitative, or discriminatory behaviour in any form is a safeguarding matter.
  • 05
    Continuous Learning
    Safeguarding training is not a one-time event. World YMCA requires regular refresher training and updates procedures in line with evolving international standards.
Policy Highlights
Designated Safeguarding Lead
Every programme or activity must have a named Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) responsible for receiving and acting on concerns.
Reporting Timeline
All safeguarding concerns must be reported to the DSL within 24 hours. Urgent cases require immediate escalation.
Confidentiality Limits
Safeguarding concerns are not subject to standard confidentiality. Safety obligations override personal privacy in all cases.
Digital Safeguarding
Online interactions with participants are governed by the same principles as in-person contact. Separate guidance is available for digital programmes.
Partner Organisations
All partner organisations delivering activities on behalf of World YMCA must confirm alignment with this policy before engagement begins.
Incident Records
All reported concerns and incidents are recorded in the confidential safeguarding register, maintained by the DSL and reviewed regularly.
Quick Reference— what to do and what to avoid
Please do
  • Complete mandatory safeguarding training before working with any participant group
  • Report any concern, disclosure, or unusual behaviour to the DSL immediately
  • Listen carefully and without judgment when someone discloses a concern
  • Follow the documented reporting procedure even when you are uncertain
  • Maintain appropriate physical and digital boundaries at all times
Please avoid
  • Investigating a concern yourself — report it and let the procedure take over
  • Promising confidentiality to someone making a disclosure
  • Contacting a participant's family without guidance from the DSL in a live case
  • Sharing case details with colleagues who are not directly involved
  • Assuming someone else has already reported — always report independently
Frequently Asked Questions
  • If you are in doubt, report it. The DSL is trained to assess the level of concern and determine the appropriate response. Erring on the side of caution is always the right approach in safeguarding.
  • No. You must not promise confidentiality in a safeguarding context. If a participant discloses something, explain calmly that you are there to support them but that you may need to share the information with someone who can help.
  • Yes. The same safeguarding principles apply in digital environments, including online communications, virtual sessions, and social media interactions with participants. The Digital Safeguarding Guidance is available on this portal.
  • The current DSL is listed on the Safeguarding section of this portal and displayed in all programme venues. You may also contact the HR department or your line manager for confirmation.

Programme Delivery Protocols
The standards and processes that help us design, deliver, and evaluate our programmes with consistency, quality, and genuine impact for every community we serve.
Section Introduction— choose your audience

This section establishes the standards and protocols that govern the design, delivery, monitoring, and evaluation of all programmes undertaken by or on behalf of World YMCA. These protocols apply to staff and volunteers involved in programme work, including activities delivered through member organisations and external partners.

The protocols cover programme planning and approval, participant eligibility, risk assessment, delivery standards, monitoring and data collection, reporting obligations, and post-programme evaluation. Adherence to these protocols ensures that all programme activity aligns with World YMCA's strategic commitments, donor requirements, and quality standards. Always refer to the current version published here.

For permanent and contract staff

This section sets out World YMCA's Programme Delivery Protocols, covering planning, approval, risk assessment, participant eligibility, monitoring, and reporting. All staff involved in programme work must follow these protocols to ensure quality, compliance, and strategic alignment. Always refer to the current version published here.

Quick reference — all staff

This section explains how World YMCA plans and runs its programmes. It covers what we expect from everyone involved in delivering activities, from how we prepare to how we follow up. If you are helping deliver a programme, please read the guidance that applies to your role.

For volunteers and partners
Key Principles
  • 01
    Values-Aligned Design
    All programmes must reflect World YMCA's core values of respect, responsibility, honesty, and care, from conception through delivery and evaluation.
  • 02
    Participant-Centred Delivery
    Programmes are designed around the needs, rights, and contexts of participants. Feedback mechanisms must be embedded at every stage of the programme cycle.
  • 03
    Risk-Aware Planning
    A formal risk assessment is required for all programmes prior to delivery. Risks must be identified, mitigated, and reviewed throughout the programme lifecycle.
  • 04
    Accountability and Transparency
    Programmes must be documented, monitored against agreed indicators, and reported on accurately. Data must not be manipulated to misrepresent outcomes.
  • 05
    Continuous Improvement
    Post-programme evaluation findings must feed into future planning. Learning is treated as a programme deliverable, not an optional add-on.
Policy Highlights
Programme Approval
All new programmes require formal sign-off from the Programme Manager before any participant-facing activity begins.
Risk Assessment
A completed risk assessment form must be submitted and approved at least five working days before in-person delivery commences.
Participant Consent
Informed consent must be obtained from all participants, or guardians where participants are minors, prior to enrolment.
Monitoring and Reporting
Programme teams must submit mid-delivery and end-of-programme reports using the standard templates available on this portal.
Partner Accountability
Partner organisations are contractually bound by these protocols and subject to the same monitoring and reporting expectations.
Data Handling
Participant data must be managed in accordance with the World YMCA Data Protection Policy and applicable local regulations.
Quick Reference— what to do and what to avoid
Please do
  • Submit your programme plan and risk assessment for approval before delivery begins
  • Collect participant consent documentation before any activity
  • Use the standard monitoring templates for all mid-delivery and final reports
  • Document any significant deviations from the approved programme plan
  • Embed a participant feedback mechanism in every programme cycle
Please avoid
  • Beginning delivery before the programme has received formal written approval
  • Modifying scope, target group, or delivery method without submitting an amendment
  • Reporting on activities that did not take place or inflating participation figures
  • Retaining participant data beyond the period in the data retention schedule
  • Engaging a partner organisation without first confirming their policy alignment
Frequently Asked Questions
  • Submit a Programme Amendment Form to your Programme Manager. Minor adjustments may be approved within five working days. Significant changes to scope, budget, or target groups require review by the senior team and, in some cases, donor notification.
  • The Programme Lead for each activity is responsible. Digital consent forms must be saved in the designated programme folder, and physical forms must be scanned and stored within 48 hours of collection.
  • The standard templates are mandatory for all internal and donor-facing reporting. If a specific donor requires a different format, the Programme Manager must approve the alternative and ensure it captures the same core data points.
  • Notify your Programme Manager immediately and document the issue and steps taken. World YMCA expects accurate and timely reporting of challenges, not just successes. Escalation protocols are outlined in the Programme Delivery Manual.

Code of Conduct & Professional Standards
The shared values and behavioural expectations that guide how we act, communicate, and represent World YMCA — in the office, in the field, and everywhere in between.
Section Introduction— choose your audience

The Code of Conduct and Professional Standards defines the behavioural and ethical expectations that apply to all staff, volunteers, and partners acting on behalf of World YMCA. These standards reflect the organisation's core values and the trust placed in all those who represent the World YMCA movement globally.

This section covers professional conduct in the workplace, external representation, use of organisational resources, social media, conflict of interest, and the handling of gifts or hospitality. All staff are required to read and comply with this code as a condition of their engagement with World YMCA. Breaches may result in disciplinary action. Always refer to the current version published here, as this document is reviewed annually.

For permanent and contract staff

This section sets out the Code of Conduct and Professional Standards for all World YMCA staff, covering workplace behaviour, external representation, resource use, social media, and conflicts of interest. Compliance is a condition of employment. Always refer to the current version published here, as the code is reviewed annually.

Quick reference — all staff

When you work or volunteer with World YMCA, you represent our values and our community. This section explains how we ask everyone to behave, both within the organisation and when speaking or acting on our behalf. Please take a moment to read what we expect and why it matters.

For volunteers and partners
Key Principles
  • 01
    Integrity in All Actions
    Staff must act with honesty and transparency in all professional dealings, including communications, decisions, and the use of organisational resources.
  • 02
    Respect for All Individuals
    Every person, whether a colleague, participant, partner, or member of the public, is entitled to respectful and non-discriminatory treatment.
  • 03
    Responsible Representation
    When speaking or acting in a professional capacity, staff represent World YMCA. Public statements must not contradict organisational positions without prior authorisation.
  • 04
    Accountability for Decisions
    Staff are accountable for decisions they make in their roles. Where guidance is unclear, escalating to a line manager is always preferable to acting unilaterally.
  • 05
    Protection of Organisational Trust
    The credibility of World YMCA depends on the conduct of everyone who works for it. Actions that damage trust, inside or outside the organisation, are a code breach.
Policy Highlights
Social Media
Staff must not post content that could bring World YMCA into disrepute, disclose confidential information, or misrepresent the organisation's positions.
Gifts and Hospitality
Gifts above a defined threshold must be declared. Accepting hospitality that could influence a professional decision is prohibited.
Use of Resources
Organisational assets, including technology, funds, and intellectual property, must be used for legitimate professional purposes only.
Whistleblowing
World YMCA protects staff who raise genuine concerns in good faith. Retaliation against a whistleblower is a serious breach of this code.
External Commitments
Board memberships, advisory roles, or public positions held outside World YMCA must be disclosed and must not create conflicts of interest.
Dress and Conduct
Professional appearance and conduct standards apply in all formal settings, including external meetings, events, and organisation-led activities.
Quick Reference— what to do and what to avoid
Please do
  • Disclose any gifts, hospitality, or potential conflicts of interest promptly
  • Speak and act with integrity in all professional settings, including informal ones
  • Use organisational resources for legitimate work purposes
  • Raise concerns through the whistleblowing procedure if you witness a serious breach
  • Seek guidance from your line manager when facing an ethical dilemma
Please avoid
  • Posting publicly in a way that could misrepresent or embarrass the organisation
  • Accepting gifts or hospitality that could influence a professional decision
  • Using your position to obtain personal benefits or commercial advantage
  • Retaliating against a colleague who raises a concern in good faith
  • Making public commitments on behalf of World YMCA without authorisation
Frequently Asked Questions
  • You may share positive news and publicly available information. However, you must not post content that contradicts official positions, discloses internal information, or could bring the organisation into disrepute. When in doubt, ask your manager before posting.
  • A conflict of interest arises when your personal interests, or those of someone close to you, could influence or appear to influence a professional decision. Common examples include evaluating a tender from a company you have a financial interest in, or managing a direct report who is a family member. Disclosure is always the correct first step.
  • Gifts with a value above CHF 50 must be declared to your line manager and recorded in the gifts register. Gifts of any value from individuals or organisations involved in active procurement decisions must be declined and reported.
  • All concerns are reviewed by a designated senior officer independent of the reported matter. The person raising the concern is protected from retaliation and, where possible, kept informed of progress. Full details are in the Whistleblowing Policy document on this portal.
